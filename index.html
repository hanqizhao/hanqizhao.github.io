About Me

I'm Hanqi ZHAO, an exchange student at the National University of Singapore (NUS). I will earn my bachelor's degree from Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST) in June 2024. My current research is about multi-agent pathfinding (MAPF) , which is finding collision-free paths for a group of agents from their location to an assigned target.

My interests and specialties are diverse: robotics, reinforcement learning, control theory, electronic engineering, mechanical engineering and computer vision. I am also a hobbyist in fashion, photography, and metal.

Research Interests

Multi-Agent Pathfinding

My research focuses on predicting the actions of neighboring agents in order to enhance collaboration among them. By training agents to generate reliable knowledge about other agents, we aim to improve inter-agent coordination and achieve more efficient pathfinding.

Wearable Robotics

I am interested in exploring wearable technologies, such as exoskeletons, to enhance collaboration between humans and machines. This has the potential to revolutionize various fields, including healthcare and labor-oriented industries.

Neuroscience

Neuroscience provides profound insights into the human brain, serving as a bridge from theory to advanced robotics engineering. This can be applied to develop technologies for substituting paralyzed limbs.

Video Object Tracking

I have developed a precise and dependable infrared video tracking model for complex scenarios with spectral and viewpoint differences.
