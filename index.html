Research Interests

Neuroscience

Neuroscience provides profound insights into the human brain, serving as a bridge from theory to advanced robotics engineering. This can be applied to develop technologies for substituting paralyzed limbs.

Brain-Computer Interface

Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) technology enables direct communication between the brain and external devices. This technology has the potential to revolutionize the way we interact with the world, and can be used to develop assistive technologies for people with disabilities.

Wearable Robotics

I am interested in exploring wearable technologies, such as exoskeletons, to offer disabled individuals barrier-free travel like ordinary people. This also has the potential to revolutionize various fields, including healthcare, sports and labor-oriented industries.

Multi-Agent Pathfinding

My research focuses on predicting the actions of neighboring agents in order to enhance collaboration among them. By training agents to generate reliable knowledge about other agents, we aim to improve inter-agent coordination and achieve more efficient pathfinding.
